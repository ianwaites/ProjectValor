Valor

Our Mission

Project Valor is a tool built for the veterans of Washington State and can act as a resource for the Washington State Department of Veteran's Affairs. Valor can be used to locate the nearest facilities to the veteran's location and displays average wait times of each facility. We did this with publicly accessible information from the VA.

This tool can be used to make sure veterans, no matter where they live, have the information to choose the location that best serves their needs. Whether it is the nearest location or the lowest wait time.

Valor can show where facilities are understaffed, where the size of the facility is not enough for the needs of veterans, and where new facilities should be built to be able to positively impact the most veterans in need.

Resources

Below is a quick list of resources that are available to you or your family. Click on the link to go to the page.

PTSD

  • Some VA centers have specialty programs for PTSD.
    https://www.ptsd.va.gov/public/treatment/therapy-med/finding-a-therapist.asp

  • Courage Beyond (formerly Not Alone) is a national non-profit organization that provides confidential, no-cost programs and services to warriors and their families facing post traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and combat stress.
    www.couragebeyond.org

  • Wounded Warrior Project® (WWP) serves veterans and service members who incurred a physical or mental injury, illness, or wound, co-incident to their military service on or after September 11, 2001 and their families.
    https://www.woundedwarriorproject.org/programs/combat-stress-recovery-program

MST

  • Military sexual trauma (MST) is the term that the Department of Veterans Affairs uses to refer to sexual assault or repeated, threatening sexual harassment that occurred while the Veteran was in the military.
    https://www.mentalhealth.va.gov/msthome.asp

  • Wounded Warrior Project® (WWP) serves veterans and service members who incurred a physical or mental injury, illness, or wound, co-incident to their military service on or after September 11, 2001 and their families.
    https://www.woundedwarriorproject.org/mst-resources

  • Need help? Call 800.656.HOPE (4673) to be connected with a trained staff member from a sexual assault service provider in your area.
    https://www.rainn.org/about-national-sexual-assault-telephone-hotline

TBI

  • BrainLineMilitary.org provides military-specific information and resources on traumatic brain injury to veterans, service members in the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, National Guard, Reserve, and their families.
    www.brainlinemilitary.org

  • DVBIC's mission is to serve active-duty military, their beneficiaries, and veterans with traumatic brain injury through state-of-the-science clinical care, innovative clinical research initiatives and educational programs, and support for force health protection services
    http://dvbic.dcoe.mil/

  • As part of the Military Health System, Defense Centers of Excellence for Psychological Health and Traumatic Brain Injury improves the lives of our nation's service members, veterans and their families by advancing excellence in psychological health and traumatic brain injury prevention and care. The DCoE offers resources for health care providers, service members, veterans and military families.
    http://www.militaryonesource.mil/footer?content_id=287825

Depression and Suicide Prevention

  • The caring responders at the Veterans Crisis Line are specially trained and experienced in helping Veterans of all ages and circumstances. Some of the responders are Veterans themselves and understand what Veterans and their families and friends have been through and the challenges Veterans of all ages and service eras face.
    https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/Resources/Additional-Information.aspx

  • Stop Soldier Suicide provides support and resources to all past and present military and their families. We do not provide direct clinical services or therapy, nor are we a crisis center.
    http://stopsoldiersuicide.org/

  • The Veterans Crisis Line connects Veterans in crisis and their families and friends with qualified, caring Department of Veterans Affairs responders through a confidential toll-free hotline, online chat, or text.
    https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/
